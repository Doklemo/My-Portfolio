From Traders to Savers

Designing a habit-forming crypto savings experience that moved impulsive traders to disciplined savers.

TL;DR
  • → Research showed 78% of Cryptobay users were trading impulsively, not building wealth
  • → Designed a goal-based savings vault with automated round-ups and lock periods
  • → Savings activation increased by 62% within 3 months of launch

The Problem

Cryptobay had a retention problem disguised as a growth problem. Users were signing up — but most were trading reactively, chasing short-term gains, and leaving when markets moved against them. The product had no mechanism to encourage long-term wealth-building behaviour.

The core insight from early discovery: users wanted to grow their money, but the product was designed for speculation, not savings.

My Role

I was the sole product designer on this project, working closely with a PM, two engineers, and a behavioural economist who joined as a consultant. I led all phases: discovery, concept development, prototyping, testing, and handoff.

Discovery & Research

I ran 14 user interviews across two segments: active traders and lapsed users who had stopped using the app. I also reviewed 6 months of in-app behaviour data with the data team.

"I keep meaning to save but when I open the app, I just end up checking prices and buying something. It happens every time." — Interview participant, 28, Lagos

The pattern was clear: the trading interface was the path of least resistance. Saving required too many manual steps and had no motivating feedback loop.

Key insight

Users didn't lack the intention to save — they lacked the structure. The solution wasn't education, it was friction reduction and automated commitment devices.

Design Process

I explored three concepts in low-fidelity: a round-up model (save spare change from trades), a vault model (locked savings goals), and a DCA model (recurring automatic buys into a savings portfolio). We tested all three with 12 users over two rounds.

The vault model won — users responded to the idea of "locking in" savings as a psychological commitment device. We layered in round-ups as a secondary feature to make saving feel effortless.

The Solution

The Cryptobay Savings Vault: a goal-based savings feature with three core mechanics:

  • Savings Goals — users set a target amount, a name, and a lock period (7, 30, or 90 days)
  • Round-ups — every trade automatically rounds up to the nearest $1 and deposits the difference into the vault
  • Streak Rewards — a visual streak counter reinforces consistent saving behaviour

Outcomes

+62% Savings activation rate
−34% Impulsive trade volume
3 mo Time to measurable impact

Reflection

This project reinforced that the best design interventions work with human psychology, not against it. The lock period was the most debated feature — some stakeholders felt it would cause churn. In testing, it did the opposite: users found commitment reassuring.

If I were to revisit this, I'd invest more in the onboarding goal-setting flow — we got it functional, but there was more to do to make it emotionally resonant.
